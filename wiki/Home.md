# Jamlog Wiki

Docs for [fast.jamlog.lol](https://fast.jamlog.lol), the Last.fm now-playing overlay you lay out in a drag-and-drop editor and paste into OBS as a browser source.

No account, no watermark, nothing saved on a server. The whole design lives in the widget URL.

## Guides

- **[Custom CSS](Custom-CSS)** (experimental): write real CSS against your widget for gradients, animations, borders, and anything else the editor doesn't have a button for.

## Getting started with the widget itself

1. Open [the editor](https://fast.jamlog.lol) and enter your Last.fm username.
2. Pick a preset, then drag things around until it looks right.
3. Hit **Add to stream →** for step-by-step instructions for OBS, Streamlabs, or XSplit, or just **Copy URL** and paste it into a browser source yourself.
4. Set the source's width and height to the size the editor recommends.

Your whole design is packed into that URL, so if you change something later, re-copy the URL and update the source. Keep it private too, since it holds your settings.

Not scrobbling yet? Last.fm has a [setup guide](https://www.last.fm/about/trackmymusic) for Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, and the rest.
